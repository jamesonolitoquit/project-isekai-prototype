# 08 — Relics, Weapons & Artifact Systems

> Project Isekai — Luxfier Alpha
> Master Reference: `plans/00_MASTER_REFERENCE.md`
> Dependencies: `02_COSMOLOGY_METAPHYSICS.md`, `04_MAGIC_SYSTEMS.md`

Integrates technological progression, crafting, alchemy, smelting, enchantments, and runic infusion
into the Luxfier framework.

---

## 8.1 Core Principles

- Artifacts exist on a spectrum of rarity, danger, and narrative importance
- Seven Relics of Virtue: high-canon, mysterious, largely inaccessible; mostly AI DM plot levers
- Seven Weapons of Sin: counterparts to Virtues, accessible only to villains or anti-heroes; extremely costly
- Crafting system: allows players to interact with lower-tier gear without breaking canon or balance
- Runic infusion: blends magic with physical items; can be dangerous if misaligned

---

---

## 8.3 Relics of Virtue

- **Chain of Patientia** — Soul-binding, restricts immortal Dreakin power. Activation limited to compatible soul wavelengths. Purpose: mostly narrative; inaccessible to players.
- **Other Six Relics** (e.g., Chalice of Aeterna, Crown of Primum, etc.) — Lost across worlds/dimensions. Can only be touched indirectly via story events, visions, or NPCs.

**Gameplay Rule:** Players may learn of them, see fragments, or encounter residual effects, but cannot wield fully.

---

## 8.4 Weapons of Sin

- Counterparts to the Seven Relics; destructive, forbidden
- Only obtainable by villains or anti-heroes under narrative control
- Costs include: physical strain, soul corruption, world-level risk
- Example: **Blade of Envy** — amplifies desire-based magic, drains sanity

## 8.6 Crafting & Alchemy Systems

### 8.6.1 Crafting Flow
1. **Material Acquisition:** mining, hunting, quest drops, rare nodes
2. **Smelting & Forging:** basic weapons and armor
3. **Alchemy:** potions, poisons, reagents (rare ingredients)
4. **Runic Infusion / Enchantment:** enhances base gear with magical properties
   - Requires matching soul wavelength or risk of corruption
   - Overcharging may permanently warp the item or user
5. **Testing & Activation:** partially unstable until used in combat or environmental magic

### 8.6.2 Runic Infusion Mechanics
- **Input:** weapon/item + rune + magic type
- **Constraints:** Soul wavelength alignment; morphing status or corruption level affects stability
- **Costs:** Mana expenditure, physical fatigue / skill requirement, potential side-effects if misaligned
- **Output:** augmented gear, with temporary or permanent boosts

### 8.6.3 Alchemy Mechanics
- **Reagent Types:** flora, minerals, monster essence, soul residue
- **Potion Effects:** healing, buff, debuff, temporary morph mitigation
- **High-Tier Alchemy:** creation of minor artifacts or components for runic infusion
- **Failure Modes:** explosion, permanent contamination, corruption residue

---

## 8.7 AI DM Enforcement Rules

- **Artifact Stability:** All crafted or infused items track integrity in the database
- **Forbidden Access:** Players cannot bypass canonical restrictions on Seven Relics or Seven Weapons
- **Historical Accuracy:** Crafting outputs reflect technological era; cannot exceed Rune Age capabilities without narrative justification
- **Corruption Monitoring:** Overcharged infusion or repeated morphing multiplies corruption cost

---

## 8.8 Integration with Other Systems

- **Belief Layer & WTOL:** Many artifacts are linked to secrets; partial knowledge triggers hints or quests
- **Factions & Politics:** Certain artifacts are faction-controlled; crafting may require guild or political favor
- **Magic Systems:** Infusion interacts with Ruin, Flux, Veil, Bind, and Life magic; costs scale with tier
- **Combat Systems:** Artifacts may alter combat resolution, but overuse may backfire

---

## Why This Layer Holds

- Introduces tangible rewards without breaking canonical balance
- Integrates technological lore, crafting, and magic seamlessly
- Supports AI DM control: rare artifacts remain rare, dangerous, and story-driven
- Enhances replayability via artifact discovery, partial knowledge, faction control, and risk-reward
