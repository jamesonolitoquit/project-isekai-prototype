# 03 — Races, Species & Ontological Biology

> Project Isekai — Luxfier Alpha
> Master Reference: `plans/00_MASTER_REFERENCE.md`
> Dependencies: `01_META_AUTHORITY.md`, `02_COSMOLOGY_METAPHYSICS.md`

Defines what entities are, what they can never escape, and how biology constrains gameplay.
Balance is enforced diegetically, not numerically.

---

## 3.1 Design Principles (Hard Constraints)

- No race is strictly superior
- Power always increases maintenance cost, not burst output
- Longevity trades off against adaptability
- Immortality always implies stagnation, dependency, or decay
- Playable ≠ safe, stable, or socially accepted

---

## 3.2 Mortal vs Immortal Classification

### 3.2.1 Mortal Races
- Finite lifespan
- High adaptability
- Faster growth curves
- Greater variance between individuals

### 3.2.2 Conditional Immortals (Playable, Restricted)
- Extended or infinite lifespan
- Fixed growth ceilings
- Mandatory upkeep conditions
- High social hostility
- Exist closer to corruption thresholds
- Succubi and Sanguinarians fall here

---

## 3.3 Elfin Race (Mortal)

**Shared Traits:** Long-lived (300–600 years), high mana affinity, low physical adaptability, cultural rigidity

### 3.3.1 Common Elfin
- Balanced magical and martial aptitude
- Strong diplomatic traditions
- Highest survivability across playstyles
- **Hidden drawback:** Slow emotional adaptation — trauma persists longer than in humans or Beastkin

### 3.3.2 High Elves
- Exceptional arcane ceilings
- High resistance to corruption
- Natural authority aura
- **Drawbacks:** Severe fertility decline, emotional detachment over time, cultural supremacism causes faction hostility, power loss during Lux-Ar instability
- **Failure mode:** Arcane atrophy if isolated from institutions

### 3.3.3 Dark Elves
- Veil magic specialists
- Superior perception manipulation
- Strong resistance to mental intrusion
- **Drawbacks:** Sunlight destabilizes Veil constructs, constant persecution, high soul-fracture risk if Veil magic overused
- **Failure mode:** Identity erosion via self-deception

---

## 3.4 Beastkin (Mortal, Hybridized)

**Shared Traits:** Adaptive physiology, elevated instincts, cultural fragmentation, universal morph ability

### 3.4.1 Morphing System (Core Mechanic)
- Morphing is: partial or full transformation, temporarily rewrites biology
- **Costs:** Accelerated aging, memory drift, instinct dominance, soul strain
- Repeated morphing permanently alters baseline form

### 3.4.2 Terrans
- Ground-based Beastkin (wolf, cat, fox, etc.)
- Strong combat instincts, enhanced senses
- **Drawbacks:** Emotional volatility, difficulty resisting corruption, territorial behavioral bias

### 3.4.3 Avians
- Winged humanoids
- Superior mobility and scouting, high spatial awareness
- **Drawbacks:** Fragile skeletal structure, vulnerable during storms or mana turbulence, cultural aversion to enclosed spaces

### 3.4.4 Merrans
- Aquatic humanoids
- Amphibious societies, deep-magic resonance
- **Drawbacks:** Surface dependency penalties, reduced fire and lightning tolerance, isolationist culture limits alliances

---

## 3.5 Succubi (Playable — High Risk)

### Ontological Nature
- Conditional immortals
- Feed on life-force / desire / emotional intensity
- Exist partially out of phase with Luxfier reality

### Core Advantages
- Extreme social manipulation
- Natural Veil and Bind synergy
- High resistance to mental domination

### Mandatory Drawbacks (Non-Negotiable)

| Drawback | Details |
|---|---|
| **Feeding Requirement** | Must periodically drain life-force. Starvation causes stat decay, reality flicker, loss of control. |
| **Corruption Proximity** | Feeding generates corruption residue. Overfeeding accelerates soul degradation. |
| **Social Hostility** | Universally distrusted. Many factions enforce kill-on-sight policies. |
| **Identity Drift** | Repeated feeding blurs genuine emotion. Long-term risk of losing selfhood. |

**Failure state:** Collapse into feral entity or Chaos echo.

---

## 3.6 Sanguinarians (Playable — Extreme Maintenance)

### Ontological Nature
- Blood-dependent immortals
- Physically anchored to Luxfier
- Soul stabilized via biological consumption

### Core Advantages
- Exceptional physical resilience
- High regeneration
- Strong resistance to death magic

### Mandatory Drawbacks

| Drawback | Details |
|---|---|
| **Blood Dependency** | Must consume sentient blood. Substitutes exist but degrade over time. |
| **Sunlight Sensitivity** | Weakens regeneration, disrupts perception, increases temporal debt. |
| **Emotional Flattening** | Reduced emotional range over centuries. Difficulty forming bonds. |
| **Stagnation Ceiling** | Growth slows dramatically after maturity. Innovation requires extreme experiences. |

**Failure state:** Blood-mad degeneration; permanent feral transformation.

---

## 3.7 Humans (Non-Playable, Canonical)

- Extremely short-lived
- Exceptional adaptability
- High narrative impact
- Near-zero presence
- **Humans are statistical anomalies, not protagonists**

---

## 3.8 Cross-Race Constraints

- Interbreeding is rare and unstable
- Hybrids suffer identity and soul coherence issues
- Kael'Vahn-level hybrids are impossible to replicate

---

## 3.9 Race Selection Constraints (Gameplay)

Succubi & Sanguinarians:
- Locked behind explicit warnings
- Reduced forgiveness thresholds
- Harsher corruption curves
- AI actively pressures upkeep mechanics
- **These races are hard mode by ontology, not numbers**

---

## Why This Holds

- Prevents powergaming immortal races
- Makes Beastkin morphing meaningful
- Keeps humans mythic without playability
- Gives AI clear failure conditions
- Encodes lore into mechanics
