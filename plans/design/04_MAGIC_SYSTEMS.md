## 4.1 Core Design Principles

- Magic is a stress on reality, not a free resource
- Every use has cost — physical, mental, or metaphysical
- Higher-tier magic always requires permanent sacrifice
- Forbidden magic exists, but consequences are severe
- Magic interacts with soul wavelength; not all spells are accessible to all characters
- Magic cannot undo cosmological authority (see `01_META_AUTHORITY.md`)

## 4.2 Magic Sources

| Source | Description | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Lux-Ar Mana** | Stabilized chaos energy filtered through the World Tree | Primary energy source for all high-tier magic |
| **Chaos Residue** | Raw, unstable chaos energy outside Luxfier | Dangerous; corruption accelerates quickly |
| **Soul Resonance** | Intrinsic power of the character's soul wavelength | Determines spell affinity and ceilings |
| **Environmental Nodes** | Ley lines, relics, or magical sites | Grants temporary amplification but consumes integrity |

## 4.3 Magic Disciplines

### 4.3.1 Ruin Magic
- Elemental destruction (Fire, Frost, Lightning, Earth)
- High physical and environmental cost
- Misuse can fracture reality
- **Forbidden Subtypes:** Planetary Ruin, Soul Burn (destroys others' souls)

### 4.3.2 Flux Magic
- Matter alteration and transmutation
- High mental strain
- Risk of permanent mutation
- **Forbidden Subtypes:** Body Swaps, Timeline Alteration

### 4.3.3 Veil Magic
- Illusions and perception manipulation
- Heavy cognitive load
- Can destabilize self-perception
- **Forbidden Subtypes:** Mind Imprint (erases personality), Reality Veil (hide entire regions)

### 4.3.4 Bind Magic
- Summoning, control, possession
- Soul-binding cost proportional to target power
- **Forbidden Subtypes:** Alpha Binding, Eternal Servitude

### 4.3.5 Life Magic
- Healing, resurrection, creation
- High metaphysical cost
- **Forbidden Subtypes:** True Resurrection (restores lost soul fully), Life Extraction (steal life essence from others)

## 4.4 Spell Cost System

Each spell has cost vectors:

| Vector | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Physical** | Fatigue, injury, aging acceleration |
| **Mental** | Memory degradation, sanity loss, perception distortions |
| **Soul / Metaphysical** | Soul strain, corruption exposure, partial soul damage |
| **Environmental** | Structural damage, instability in surrounding reality |

**Rule:** The AI DM must enforce costs automatically; failure to pay cost results in consequences escalating along Corruption Progression (from `02_COSMOLOGY_METAPHYSICS.md`).

---

## 4.5 Spell Learning & Progression

- **Soul Wavelength Affinity:** Determines accessible disciplines and tier ceilings
- **Experience Scaling:** Spell mastery improves efficiency but increases risk of catastrophic failure if thresholds exceeded
- **Forbidden Knowledge:** Only AI DM can unlock through narrative events; attempting forbidden spells without guidance triggers reality backlash

---

## 4.6 Spellcasting Mechanics

1. **Declaration** — Player states intent
2. **Complexity Check** — AI DM evaluates feasibility, risk, and cost
3. **Roll/Resolution** — Outcome determined via Resolution Engine
4. **Cost Enforcement** — Apply costs and consequences
5. **World Impact** — Update Belief Layer, WTOL, and Playthrough State

---

## 4.7 Forbidden Practices

| Practice | Consequence | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Soul Burn** | Partial or full soul destruction | Only AI-approved villains may attempt |
| **Body Swap / Possession** | Risk of permanent identity loss | Cross-canonical entities forbidden |
| **Mind Imprint** | Permanent personality overwrite | AI DM vetoes for players |
| **Reality Veil** | Hide / erase entire regions | Violates Cosmology Layer |
| **Alpha Binding** | Permanent control of immortal or near-immortal | Only AI-authorized NPCs |
| **True Resurrection** | Full restoration of a lost soul | Only narrative villains may attempt |
| **Life Extraction** | Drain life essence from others | Major corruption spike |

**Rule:** Players can observe hints of these, but cannot access them without story-driven AI permission.

---

## 4.8 High-Risk Magic Indicators

- **Forbidden Trace:** Presence of residual chaos or corruption
- **Soul Overload:** Overextension triggers automatic backfire
- **Environmental Feedback:** Lux-Ar or world nodes resist reality stress

---

## 4.9 Cross-Race Effects

- Beastkin morphing interacts with Flux and Life magic: increases cost exponentially
- Succubi & Sanguinarians: magic costs double unless feeding requirements met
- Humans: can attempt magic but fail high-tier spells, permanent strain if misused

---

## 4.10 AI DM Enforcement Notes

- Always evaluate affinity ceiling before resolving
- Apply cost immediately; never allow "risk-free" experimentation
- Keep forbidden practices opaque unless narrative-driven
- Escalate failure according to Corruption Progression
